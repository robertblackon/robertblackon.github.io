The Anatomy of Breathing

Benefits:

  • Improve your health.
  • Obtain better control of your body and mind.
  • Help yourself relax and calm your body and mind and emotions.
  • Control your muscle tension and develop better muscle tone.
  • Give your nervous system a tonic.
  • Better manage pain and discomfort.
  • Reduce your stress.
  • Experience what it feels like to breathe without interference.
  • Enhance your overall performance.

To live is to breathe: breathing is a more fundamental process than eating or drinking. We can go forty or more days without food, maybe three days without water, but no more than seconds or a couple of minutes without air. Breath is our constant companion and intimate experience of living. We affect it as it in tum affects us. Our first communication as babies is through the wail of our breath and it continues to form our words and communication until we depart this body.
